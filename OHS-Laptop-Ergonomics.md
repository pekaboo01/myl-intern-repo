1. What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?

Using a laptop alone often causes poor posture because the screen is too low and the keyboard is attached to it. This can lead to neck strain, back pain, shoulder tension, and wrist discomfort. Over time, it may cause long-term posture problems and repetitive strain injuries.

2. What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop?

A laptop stand can raise the screen to eye level. An external keyboard and mouse allow hands to stay in a natural position. An ergonomic chair with back support helps maintain good posture. An external monitor can also improve screen height and reduce neck strain.

3. What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup?

The top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level. The chair should support the lower back, and feet should rest flat on the floor. Elbows should be at about a 90-degree angle when typing. The keyboard and mouse should be close enough to avoid reaching forward.

4. What daily habits reduce the impact of prolonged laptop use?

Taking short movement breaks, stretching, sitting upright, checking posture regularly, and following the 20-20-20 rule for eye strain (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds) can reduce discomfort.
