1. What are the main Agile ceremonies, and what is their purpose?

*Daily Stand-ups*
Daily stand-ups are short meetings where team members share what they worked on, what they plan to do next, and if they have any blockers. The purpose is to keep everyone aligned and aware of progress. It helps identify issues early and encourages accountability.

*Sprint Planning (Scrum) vs. Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)*
In Scrum, Sprint Planning happens at the beginning of each sprint. The team selects tasks they commit to completing within a fixed time period. The purpose is to define a clear sprint goal and plan the work ahead.

In Kanban, there are no fixed sprints. Instead, prioritization happens continuously. Tasks are added, reordered, or pulled based on urgency and capacity. The purpose is to maintain flexibility and ensure the most important work is always being handled.

*Retrospectives*
Retrospectives are meetings where the team reflects on what went well, what did not go well, and what can be improved. The purpose is continuous improvement. It allows the team to adjust processes and improve collaboration.

*Backlog Refinement*
Backlog refinement is when the team reviews upcoming tasks, clarifies requirements, and ensures they are ready to be worked on. The purpose is to avoid confusion and ensure tasks are clear before development begins.

2. How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?

Agile teams collaborate asynchronously using tools like issue trackers, Kanban boards, shared documents, and communication platforms such as Slack or email. Updates are written clearly so others can read them later. This reduces the need for everyone to be online at the same time.

Teams working across time zones rely on clear documentation, detailed task descriptions, recorded meetings, and well-updated boards. This ensures that work continues smoothly even when some team members are offline. Good written communication becomes very important in this setup.

1. How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?

Agile ceremonies create regular opportunities for communication. Daily stand-ups keep everyone informed about progress and blockers. Planning and prioritization meetings ensure that the team is working on the right tasks. Retrospectives allow the team to improve how they work together.

These ceremonies prevent misunderstandings and keep everyone aligned with goals and expectations. Since communication is continuous, problems are addressed early instead of becoming bigger issues later.
