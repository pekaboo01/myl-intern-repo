1. What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?

*Developers* are responsible for building and maintaining the product. They write code, fix bugs, and implement new features. Their main goal is to create working software that meets requirements.

*QA (Quality Assurance)* ensures that the product works correctly and meets quality standards. QA tests features, finds bugs, writes automated test scripts, and verifies that issues are fixed properly. QA helps maintain product stability and reliability.

*Product Manager (PM)* is responsible for defining what needs to be built. The PM manages priorities, gathers requirements, communicates with stakeholders, and ensures that the team is building features that provide value to users.

*Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)* focuses on workflow, team coordination, and delivery timelines. They help remove blockers, track progress, and ensure the team follows Agile principles. They support communication and keep work organized.

*Stakeholders*, who provide direction and business goals.

*UX Designers*, who focus on user experience and interface design.

*Customer Support*, who relay user feedback and report recurring issues from real users.

2. How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum?

In Scrum, collaboration happens within structured sprints. The team plans work at the beginning of the sprint and commits to completing it. Roles are clearly defined, and there are regular meetings such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives. Everyone works toward sprint goals within a fixed timeframe.

In Kanban, collaboration is more continuous and flexible. There are no fixed sprints. Tasks flow through a board, and team members pull tasks when ready. Communication still happens regularly, but it is less structured compared to Scrum. Kanban focuses more on managing workflow and limiting work in progress rather than planning in time-based cycles.

Scrum is structured and time-based, while Kanban is flow-based and adaptable.
